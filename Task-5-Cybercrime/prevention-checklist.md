# **Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist**



Being active online is part of college life, but staying safe is just as important. Here’s a practical checklist to protect yourself from cyber threats:

* Use strong and unique passwords

Avoid using the same password for multiple accounts. Combine letters, numbers, and       symbols for better security.



* Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)

Always turn on 2FA for apps like email, social media, and banking to add an extra layer of protection.



* Never share OTPs or banking details

No genuine bank or service will ask for your OTP, UPI PIN, or passwords—never share them with anyone.



* Be cautious with UPI payment requests

Always verify before approving any “Collect Request” on apps like Google Pay, PhonePe, or Paytm. Scammers often trick users into sending money instead of receiving it.



* Avoid public Wi-Fi for financial transactions

Do not use open Wi-Fi networks for logging into bank accounts or making payments, as they can be easily compromised.



* Check links before clicking

Don’t click on suspicious links sent via SMS, email, or WhatsApp. Phishing links often look real but steal your data.



* Limit personal information sharing

Avoid posting sensitive details like phone numbers, addresses, or college IDs publicly on social media.



* Install apps only from trusted sources

Download apps only from official stores like Google Play Store or Apple App Store to avoid malware.



* Regularly update your devices and apps

Updates often include security fixes that protect your data from new threats.



* Log out from shared devices

Always log out after using your accounts on public or shared computers, especially in labs or libraries.



#### **Report Cyber Crime Immediately**



If you face any cyber fraud or suspicious activity, report it without delay:

##### National Cyber Crime Portal: https://cybercrime.gov.in⁠ 

##### Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930

Quick reporting can help prevent financial loss and increase the chances of recovery.
