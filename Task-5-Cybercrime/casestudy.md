# Case Study: Identity Theft in a College Student’s Life



Niya is a college student in her second year. Her brother thought that she would never have guessed that a single e-mail could lead to a grave problem. One fine day, she received an e-mail which appeared to be from her bank asking her to “verify” her account information in exchange of suspicion activities. Without any second thought she clicked the link and fed in her log-in information.



This is how identity theft often starts. Firstly, the attacker will send a phishing email or a message which is well masked. Then the victim will end up giving essential information such as passwords, one time passwords (OTPs), Card details or even personal information. Thirdly, the attacker will use these details to open bank accounts, social media accounts or requesting for a loan in the victim‘s name.



In the case of Niya, a few days after, her bank account was emptied and her identity was used to make several online shopping transactions. She only found out when she received notifications of transactions. Unfortunately, it was already too late.



Most common victims are students, old people and all those not used to digital safety, as these people might not be able to detect a scam message. The end result could be disastrous: financial loss, tainted credit record, psychic stress and a long journey to prove who you really are again.

Finally, she had to inform her bank and the cybercrime authority, shut down her accounts and spend weeks resolving the issue. This example can be used to demonstrate what significant damage a very simple slip on the internet can do and show how important being cautious is.

In 2017, Equifax Data Breach exposed data of 147 million people, including Social Security numbers, enabling widespread identity theft and financial fraud (Source: Equifax reports).
